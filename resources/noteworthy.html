News and Noteworthy

The Plantage carries a wide variety of plants, many of which can be found in the pages of your favorite magazines. If your customers read about it, you've got to know where to get it. Check it out.

Home Magazine (April 2002)

  • "Oak-leaved Hydrangea: A Shrub for All Seasons," page 40
    The Plantage carries Hydrangea quercifolia "Snow Queen," which is mentioned in the article.

Garden Gate magazine (April 2002)

  • "Go With Green," page 12
    The Plantage carries Hydrangea "Annabelle," featured on page 13.

  • "A Tapestry of Sedum," page 16
    The Plantage carries Sedum kamtschaticum, recommended on page 17, and Sedum spathulifolium "Cape Blanco," recommended on page 18.

  • "Editor's Choice—European Wild Ginger," page 42
    The Plantage grows Asarum europaeum, given special mention on page 42.

Country Living Gardener (April 2002)

  • The Plantage can supply Coreopsis rosea "Sweet Dreams," featured in "the Cutting Edge," page 12.

  • "Fern Mania" on page 72 describes Athyrium nipponicum "Pictum" (Japanese Painted Fern) and Osmunda cinnamomea (Cinnamon Fern), both of which the Plantage raises, as well as many other varieties.

  • "Potrait of a Garden," on page 64, explains transformation of "ordinary lawn" to "extraordinary garden." The Plantage raises many of the plants described in the article, such as Lysimachia nummularia "Aurea," and full lines of Aquilegia hybrid, Coreopsis and Euphorbia.
