Grasses That Love Wet Feet

Ornamental Grasses are found worldwide and enjoy a special place in the landscape, providing year-round interest as well as texture and grace. The grasses noted below are versatile, in that they will grow in moist to wet conditions as well as in drier situations.

All are hardy to zones 5-9 unless otherwise noted:

  • Arrhenatherum elatius: Bulbous Oat Grass. Moist, well-drained soil.

  • Briza media: Perennial Quaking Grass. Moist meadows, praries.

  • Calamagrostis acutiflora "Stricta": Feather Reed Grass. Moist meadows.

  • Carex glauca: Blue Sedge. Wet to dry fields.

  • Carex morrowii "Variegata": Silver Variegated Japanese Sedge. Moist places.

  • Carex nigra: Black Flowering Sedge. Wet soils.

  • Chasmanthium latifolium: Northern Sea Oats. Streamsides.

  • Deschampsia caespitosa: Tufted Hairgrass. Wet areas.

  • Deshampsia flexuosa: Crinkled Hairgrass. Moist meadows.

  • Hakonechloa macra: Hakone Grass. Streamsides.

  • Imperata cylindrica "Red Baron": Jap. Blood Grass - Moist, sandy streamsides. Zones 6-9.

  • Juncus effusu: Soft Rush. Wet areas, marshes, streamsides.

  • Miscanthus giganteus: Giant Chinese Silver Grass. Streamsides.

  • Miscanthus sinensis: Japanese Silver Grass. Moist streamsides, meadows.

  • Miscanthus sinensis var. strictus: Porcupine Grass. Moist, fertile areas.

  • Molinia caerulea: Moor Grass. Boggy heaths, seasonally wet meadows.

  • Molinia cae. Arundinacea: Tall Moor Grass. Wet moorlands, boggy heaths.

  • Pennisetum alopecuroides: Fountain Grass. Streamsides. Zones 6-9.

  • Phalaris arundinacea "Dwarf Garters": Ribbon Grass. Streamsides, moist meadows.

  • Phalaris arun. "Feesey's Form": Ribbon Grass. Rich, moist soil.

  • Phalaris arun. Picta: Ribbon Grass. Moist areas, streamsides.
