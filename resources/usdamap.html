This is the United States Department of Agriculture's Plant Hardiness Zone Map, Miscellaneous Publication 1475, denoting the various minimum-heat zones within the North American continent. This 1990 revision supersedes USDA Miscellaneous Publication 814, Plant Hardiness Zone Map, which was last revised in 1965.

All zone designations represent the lowest temperature zone where that particular plant can reasonably be expected to survive the winter. Areas falling within zones 10b and 11 are essentially frost-free year 'round. Keep in mind that certain microclimates exist within each particular garden where exposure to light and wind, soil types, and snow cover may affect winter hardiness.
